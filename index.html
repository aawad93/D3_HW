Correlations Discovered Between Obesity and Income

A strong correlation is observed between a state's income and the state's obesity level. States that have a higher income have a lot less obese people than states with lower incomes.

All states that have an obesity percentage of higher than 30% all fall under the income level of 60 thousand.

Only Maryland and Alaska have an obesity rate that is close to 30% but still have a high income. More factors might be goo to look is weather and popular cuisine of the area. Also, interesting to note that Colorado has the lowest obesity rate, and West Virginia has the highest rate.
